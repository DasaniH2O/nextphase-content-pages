Field Guide · LLM Cost · as of 20 Jul 2026

One cache hit repays a 5-minute write. Two repay the hour.

Prompt caching stores the static front of your prompt so later calls read it back at a tenth of the price. You pay a premium on the first write — so the only question that matters is how many reuses it takes to get your money back. Here are the exact break-even points, the per-model token floor that silently switches caching off, and the dollar math on Opus 4.8.

The centerpiece · break-even by cache type

All multiples are of base input price. “Saved per hit” = 1.0× (what a full reprocess would cost) − 0.10× (the hit). Anthropic Claude API, as of 20 Jul 2026.

break-even hits = ceil( write_premium ÷ 0.90 )
5m: ceil(0.25 ÷ 0.90) = 1  ·  1h: ceil(1.00 ÷ 0.90) = 2

The floor that silently turns caching off

Below a minimum shared-prefix length, nothing caches — and there is no error. You just quietly pay full price. The floor depends on the model:

Minimum cacheable prefixModels
512 tokensFable 5, Mythos 5
1,024 tokensOpus 4.8, Sonnet 5, Sonnet 4.6 / 4.5
2,048 tokensOpus 4.7
4,096 tokensOpus 4.6 / 4.5, Haiku 4.5

Anthropic prompt-caching docs, as of 20 Jul 2026.

Get a hit every time — three rules

1
Put the static content first. Order is tools → system → messages. The cache breakpoint has to sit after the last byte-identical block, so anything variable must come after it.
2
Keep the prefix byte-identical. A timestamp, an edited tool schema, a toggled image or citation setting anywhere before the breakpoint invalidates everything downstream and forces a fresh full-price write.
3
Clear the floor. Confirm your shared prefix beats the token minimum for your model (above). Verify with usage.cache_read_input_tokens in the response — if it’s 0, you’re paying full price.

One honest limitation

The 5-minute default TTL refreshes free on every hit — but if your traffic is slow or bursty and a gap exceeds five minutes, the cache expires and your next call pays the 1.25× write again, not the 0.10× read. Caching rewards steady, high-reuse workloads; it can quietly cost you more on sporadic ones. Measure before you assume 70% savings.

How to switch it on

Anthropic — add to a content block:

"cache_control":
  { "type": "ephemeral" }

Default TTL 5 min. Add "ttl":"1h" for the hour. OpenAI needs nothing — check cached_tokens in usage.
