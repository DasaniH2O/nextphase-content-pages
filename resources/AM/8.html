Claude Fable 5 · silent routing

Your Fable 5 isn’t dumber — a safety router is quietly serving you Opus 4.8

Since the July 1 reinstatement, a classifier sits in front of Fable 5. On anything security- or debugging-flavored it silently reroutes you to the weaker model. You pay for Fable, you get the fallback. Here’s how to tell, and what to do.

What’s actually happening

A new safety classifier was bolted on as a condition of Fable’s return. It watches every prompt; when it reads something as security-adjacent or coding/debugging, it reroutes the request to Opus 4.8 instead of Fable 5 — without telling you. So a “worse” Fable is usually a misrouted one.

The receipts (BridgeBench, after Jul 1)

The tell: on the debugging set, only 3 of 12 tasks actually reached Fable 5 — the other 9 were rerouted to Opus 4.8 and scored zero. That collapse is routing, not capability.

Are you affected?

  • Your prompt contains vulnerability, exploit, hook, memory management, or similar terms
  • You’re debugging or refactoring real code
  • Blind human-preference (non-code) barely moved — document +34, expert text +25 — so casual writing users won’t notice

What to do right now

1
Verify which model answered. Ask the model to state its name/version at the end of a suspect response.
2
De-trigger the prompt. Rephrase security/debugging asks in neutral terms; split the sensitive word out of the first message.
3
Benchmark your own tasks before vs after on a fixed set — don’t trust vibes, the effect is task-specific.

Why it exists

Amazon researchers found a jailbreak getting Fable to demonstrate software vulnerabilities → a U.S. export-control ban → Fable was reinstated on condition of this classifier. It casts too wide a net.

Straight talk

Anthropic acknowledged the false positives but gave no timeline to tune it down. So plan around it, don’t wait for a fix.
