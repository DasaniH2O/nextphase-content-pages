Route routine work to open models, reserve the frontier for high-stakes calls

The argument that open-weight AI wins is about money, not ideology: everyone who isn’t selling a frontier model profits when models are cheap and swappable, so they fund and adopt open weights — “commoditize your complement.” The checkable backbone is the mid-2026 shortlist below, sitting within single-digit points of the closed frontier on coding and reasoning.

The verdict

Four of the five strongest open leaders are Chinese labs, and on everyday coding and reasoning the gap to the Western closed frontier is now single digits. For most production work, an open model is good enough and far cheaper — but closed labs still lead on the hardest agentic-coding benchmarks (SWE-Bench Pro) and long-horizon tasks. The winning move isn’t all-open or all-closed. It’s tiered.

The routing rule you can apply today

Send to open / cheapRoute here by default

  • Classification, extraction, summarization
  • Bulk or high-volume calls where cost dominates
  • Drafts a human reviews before shipping
  • Self-host / data-residency requirements

Reserve the frontierPay up when it matters

  • Hardest agentic coding & long multi-step tasks
  • High-stakes calls with no human check
  • Anything where a single wrong answer is expensive
  • Newest capability that lands closed-first

This is a thesis, not a factIt predicts an outcome. The strongest counter: closed labs keep a real, recurring lead — frontier capability tends to arrive months ahead, and they hold an edge on the hardest agentic-coding benchmarks and can absorb capex an open ecosystem can’t coordinate. So the honest read is tiered coexistence, not winner-take-all.
