Template · AI Serial Video

The transferable asset isn’t the model — it’s a three-beat template that forces a part two.

A viral case study paired an AI text-to-video clip with a repeatable script and paywalled the sequels. Strip out the hype and one thing carries: the story shape. Here it is, ready to paste into any writing model, with a worked example and the real cost math.

The three-beat template

1

Recognizable cast

Open on faces or figures the viewer already knows in the first two seconds. Familiarity buys the watch before the story earns it.

SLOT: [two well-known figures the audience recognizes on sight]
2

One absurd conflict

Exactly one impossible situation — not two. The single clean premise is what makes the clip screenshot-able and easy to describe to a friend.

SLOT: [one impossible situation those figures are forced into]
3

Cliffhanger that demands the sequel

End on the frame before the resolution. The unanswered question is the entire business model — it’s what a viewer will pay to see resolved.

SLOT: [the exact moment you cut — right before the payoff]

Paste this into your writing model

# Serial clip script — fill the three slots, keep the order
CAST:      [two recognizable figures]
CONFLICT:  [one absurd situation they're forced into]
CUT ON:    [the frame right before resolution]

Write a 15-second shot list for beat 2, photoreal, single
location. End the script on CUT ON as an open question.
Title = the question. First comment = "Part 2?"

Worked example

Cast: two famous rival footballers.

Conflict: they’re handcuffed together and have to win a 2-v-11 match to be released.

Cut on: the ref blows the whistle for a last-minute penalty — screen ends before the kick.

Episode 1 posts free for reach; the penalty outcome is Episode 2, behind the sub.

The number the tool part hides

  • Fanvue takes 20% off the top — gross is not net
  • Seedance 2.0’s own API was not publicly open (Mar 2026); you route through resellers
  • AI content must be disclosed on Fanvue
  • Claude writes the script — it does not make the money

The honest catch

The headline “$3,678 in a week, ~614 subscribers, 2M views” is one creator’s self-reported figure from a single X post — unverified. Treat it as a possibility ceiling, not a forecast. What’s verifiable is the template and the tool costs above; the earnings are not.
